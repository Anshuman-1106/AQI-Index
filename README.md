# AQI-Index
Basic AQI Index visual of Indian states and cities
KEY FINDINGS & INSIGHTS
National Trends:
•	Average AQI declined from 156.5 (2015) to 102.8 (2025), indicating measurable improvement over the decade
•	COVID-19 lockdown (2020) caused a sharp AQI drop to 113.7, the lowest recorded year at that point
•	PM10 is the most dominant pollutant (44% of records), followed by PM2.5 (28%) and O3 (7%)
Air Quality Distribution:
•	36.5% of days recorded Satisfactory AQI; 32.6% Moderate — together accounting for ~70% of all readings
•	14.5% of days were Poor or worse — indicating serious pollution episodes, primarily in winter months
•	Only 0.6% (2,567 records) reached Severe category, concentrated in northern states
Regional Analysis:
•	Delhi is the most polluted state with average AQI of 215.9 — nearly double the national average
•	Bihar (165.9), Himachal Pradesh (160.1), and Uttar Pradesh (159.5) follow as highly polluted states
•	Uttarakhand has a relatively better avg AQI of 86.9; Dehradun specifically averages 96.7 (Moderate)
•	Southern and coastal states generally report lower AQI compared to the Indo-Gangetic Plain

PROBLEM STATEMENT
Air pollution is a critical public health issue in India. Despite being one of the world's most populated countries, there was a lack of accessible, visual tools to understand AQI patterns at the state and city level over time. Policymakers, researchers, and citizens had limited means to:
•	Identify which regions are most affected and during which seasons
•	Track whether government interventions and policies have improved air quality over the years
•	Pinpoint the primary pollutants driving poor air quality in specific regions
•	Compare air quality trends across states and cities in a single, unified view
